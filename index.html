Patient History & Physical Exam

A 58-year-old male presents to the emergency department complaining of severe, crushing substernal chest pain that began 45 minutes ago while shoveling snow. The pain radiates to his left jaw and shoulder and is accompanied by diaphoresis, dyspnea, and mild nausea. He has a history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and a 30 pack-year smoking history.

Vitals: BP: 145/92 mmHg, HR: 98 bpm, RR: 22 bpm, SpO2: 94% on room air.
